Elif Korkmaz

PhD Student in Geography | GIScience & Multi-Hazard Vulnerability Research

I'm a PhD student in Geography at the University of Iowa. My research examines how social vulnerability interacts with multi-hazard processes. Existing vulnerability frameworks are largely static and place-based — relying on aggregated census data and fixed spatial units — which limits their ability to capture temporal dynamics, spatial dependence, and scale-dependent variation in risk patterns under hazard events. To address these limitations, my research draws on GIScience and spatial analysis methods to conceptualize vulnerability as a dynamic process that evolves across disaster phases and is reshaped by spatially and/or temporally clustered hazards, with the aim of better explaining cumulative risk and unequal vulnerability outcomes in multi-hazard contexts.
