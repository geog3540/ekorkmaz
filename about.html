Elif Korkmaz

I’m a PhD student in Geography at the University of Iowa. My work focuses on social vulnerability and multi-hazard processes, using GIScience and spatial analysis to understand how risk and vulnerability evolve across space and time. I’m especially interested in the ways hazards compound, how vulnerability changes across disaster phases, and how spatial dependence and scale shape unequal outcomes. Professionally, I enjoy building clear, reproducible geospatial workflows and communicating results through effective maps and visualizations. Outside of research, I love learning new tools, exploring places, and staying curious about the human and environmental processes that shape the landscapes we live in.
