Flow Map: South American Banana Trade, 2019

Static flow map showing major exporter/importer relationships and net trade patterns across South America.

Download Flow Map Project (JSON)

Interpretation

Places that Send and Receive the Most Flows

Ecuador is the dominant sender, exporting 476,001 tonnes — far more than any other country. Its largest flows go toward Argentina, Chile, and Uruguay. Argentina is the largest importer (433,273 tonnes), receiving flows from Ecuador, Bolivia, Paraguay, and Brazil. Chile is the second biggest importer, sourced almost entirely from Ecuador. Uruguay imports 50,857 tonnes from Brazil and Ecuador.

General Direction of Flows

The dominant flow direction is strongly north to south. The largest arrows originate from Ecuador in the northwest and point toward the southern countries of Argentina, Chile, and Uruguay. Bananas grow in tropical and subtropical zones in the north but not in the temperate south. A secondary west-to-east flow exists from Bolivia to Argentina, and Brazil sends smaller flows to Uruguay and Argentina along the Atlantic coast.

Flow Patterns in Relation to Node Symbols and Choropleth Base Map

The choropleth reinforces flow patterns — yellow-green countries (Ecuador, Bolivia, Paraguay, Brazil) are net exporters and serve as flow origins; blue-shaded countries (Argentina, Chile, Uruguay) are net importers and flow destinations. The proportional gray node circles add detail: Ecuador and Argentina have similarly large circles, showing both are highly active traders despite being on opposite ends of the net trade spectrum. Brazil's small circle relative to its size suggests most production is for domestic use.
